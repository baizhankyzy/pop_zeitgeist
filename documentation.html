Documentation

The Pop Zeitgeist project extracts, scrapes, and analyses song lyrics and related metadata from 2000 to 2010 with the goal of analysing and visualising patterns in popular culture. This comprehensive study, also known as macroanalysis, enables us to categorise and understand song lyrics on a wide scale. As defined by Matthew L. Jockers in his book Macroanalysis, “the macroanalytic approach is simply another method of gathering details, bits of information that may have escaped our attention because of their sheer multitude. At a more sophisticated level, it is about accessing details that are otherwise unavailable, forgotten, ignored, or impossible to extract.”

After selecting and gathering data, we used Python packages like Pandas and Matplotlib to analyse our metadata. We visualised the results of scraping the lyrics in order to gain insights about gender, region, emotions, and frequently used terms, among other factors. By examining these aspects, the project seeks to uncover patterns and shifts in popular music over time. Our analysis covers thorough procedures for data collection, preparation, and visualisation, as well as advanced natural language processing (NLP) techniques and statistical tools used to create insightful and entertaining visualisations. These algorithmic techniques helped us accomplish our goal of analysing and interpreting the tunes.

The idea of using algorithmic tools in the process of interpreting data in a more critical sense, also explained by Stephen Ramsay that “we create tools—practical, instrumental, verifiable mechanisms—that enable critical engagement, interpretation, conversation, and contemplation. It proposes that we channel the heightened objectivity made possible by the machine into the cultivation of those heightened subjectivities necessary for critical work.” This quote emphasises the significance of applying algorithmic tools, such as natural language processing (NLP) techniques and statistical analysis, to comprehend huge datasets like song lyrics. These technologies offer a methodical and objective approach to dealing with large amounts of data, revealing patterns and subtleties that might otherwise go unnoticed. This allows for a more nuanced and critical engagement with the data, resulting in deeper insights and interpretations of popular music patterns. This is consistent with Ramsay's notion of harnessing robots' objectivity to improve human subjective understanding and critical study of cultural processes.

The main research question of our project is to analyse how popular music over time has evolved in different dimensions.
